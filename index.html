Dr. Kwame Nkrumah

The man who gave Ghana independence.

Timeline of Dr. Kwame Nkrumah's Life

  • 1909 – Born in Nkroful, Gold Coast (now Ghana).
  • 1930 – Enrolls at Achimota School in Accra for teacher training.
  • 1935 – Moves to the United States to study at Lincoln University in Pennsylvania.
  • 1943 – Earns a Master’s degree in Philosophy and Political Science from the University of Pennsylvania.
  • 1945 – Moves to London and becomes involved in Pan-African activism, co-organizing the 5th Pan-African Congress in Manchester.
  • 1947 – Returns to the Gold Coast at the invitation of the United Gold Coast Convention (UGCC) as its General Secretary to campaign for independence.
  • 1948 – Arrested following nationwide protests against British rule but gains widespread public support.
  • 1949 – Breaks away from the UGCC to form the Convention People’s Party (CPP), calling for immediate independence.
  • 1950 – Imprisoned by the British for launching the "Positive Action" campaign, a series of strikes and protests.
  • 1951 – Wins a landslide victory while still in prison and is released to become the Leader of Government Business.
  • 1952 – Becomes Prime Minister of the Gold Coast under British rule.
  • 1957 – Leads the Gold Coast to independence, renaming it Ghana, becoming its first Prime Minister. Declares: "The independence of Ghana is meaningless unless it is linked up with the total liberation of Africa."
  • 1960 – Becomes Ghana’s first President after the country becomes a republic.
  • 1961 – Launches ambitious industrial and infrastructure projects, including the Akosombo Dam.
  • 1963 – Helps establish the Organization of African Unity (OAU) in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia.
  • 1964 – Declares Ghana a one-party state, with himself as "President for Life".
  • 1966 – Overthrown in a military coup while on a trip to China and North Vietnam. Goes into exile in Guinea, where he is named Co-President.
  • 1972 – Dies in Bucharest, Romania, after battling illness. His body is later returned to Ghana and buried at the Kwame Nkrumah Mausoleum in Accra.
